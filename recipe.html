Kheer is a traditional Indian dessert made with rice, milk, and sugar. It's rich, creamy, and flavored with cardamom, nuts, and saffron. Prefect for festivals or any sweet cravings!

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup basmasti rice
  • 1 liter full cream milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar (according to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon of ghee
  • 5-6 strands of saffron
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cardamom power
  • 1-2 teaspoon of chopped nuts
  • 1-2 tablespoon, golden raisins

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice well and soak it in water for 20-30 minutes.
  2. In a deep pan, bring the milk to a gentle boil.
  3. Add the soaked rice and stir continuously to avoid sticking.
  4. Cook on low flame until the rice becomes soft and the milk thickens.
  5. Add sugar and mix well until it dissolves completely.
  6. Stir in cardamom powder, dry fruits, and saffron strands.
  7. Simmer for 2-3 minutes and turn off the heat.
  8. Serve warm or chilled, garnished with nuts!
